Description

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is a beloved Indian dish known for its rich and creamy tomato-based sauce. Tender pieces of marinated chicken are simmered in a buttery gravy with fragrant spices, creating a deeply satisfying meal that’s best enjoyed with naan or rice.

This recipe offers an easy and approachable way to recreate this restaurant favorite at home. The balance of spices, cream, and tomatoes makes it irresistible to both newcomers and seasoned Indian food lovers alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger
  • 1 (15 oz) can tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)
  • Cooked basmati rice or naan for serving

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, cumin, garam masala, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Add chicken pieces and marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for best flavor).
  2. Heat butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until soft and golden brown.
  3. Add garlic and ginger and cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the tomato sauce and simmer for 10–15 minutes, until thickened slightly.
  5. In a separate pan, cook the marinated chicken until browned and mostly cooked through. Add to the sauce.
  6. Pour in the heavy cream and stir to combine. Simmer everything together for 10 more minutes until chicken is fully cooked and the sauce is creamy and rich.
  7. Adjust salt to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
  8. Serve hot with basmati rice or warm naan.
